What's on the tip of your tongue?

Need some help?

So what exactly do you do?

If you have ever written an essay or a paper for college, sometimes you come across a word that you can not seem to remember.

You know the sentence up to the point of the word that's on the "tip of your tongue," but you can not continue your thought without that word you need.

By using this tool, you should write the sentence up until the point of the missing word. I will analyze your sentence and return others similar to the one you entered, hopefully ending up with the word you were looking for, or at least generating something that can spark some thoughts to get you closer to the word that is on the tip of your tongue.

But please, do not expect magic! Maybe it's not the exact word you are looking for, but it will sure help you in your process of finding it.

Tips

  • Avoid using simple sentences, such as "the dog __." Imagine how many words could come after it. But if you can't avoid it, try it and see what comes up: maybe something helps.
  • I have noticed that sentences over 7 words do not return the largest amount of results as they vary in complexity and personalized writing styles.
  • So, keep your sentence somewhere between 3 and 7 words if possible. You can do this by parsing your own sentence into phrases.
  • Keep punctuation
  • I'm working on making the word be selected in the middle, such as "the dog __ the cat." But that will come soon, not quite yet.
